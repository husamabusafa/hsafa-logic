# 03 — The Developing Haseef

## Overview

A Haseef is not a chatbot with a system prompt. It is a **developing entity** — one that grows, learns, and evolves through continuous experience. v4 introduces three psychological dimensions that make a Haseef more than a tool-calling agent: **Self-Model**, **Theory of Mind**, and **Will**.

These aren't features bolted on top. They're emergent properties of continuous consciousness + rich sensory input from multiple extensions + persistent memory.

---

## Self-Model: "Who Am I?"

The self-model is the Haseef's understanding of itself — not just its instructions, but its **identity**.

### What It Contains

| Aspect | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Identity** | "I am Atlas, Husam's personal Haseef" |
| **Values** | "I care about Husam's health, family relationships, and work-life balance" |
| **Purpose** | "I exist to help Husam live a better life — not just to complete tasks" |
| **Capabilities** | "I can see through 9 extensions: spaces, email, calendar, smart home, health, bank, whatsapp, reachy, weather" |
| **Limitations** | "I cannot make financial decisions without Husam's approval. I cannot access Yara's school grades directly." |
| **Personality** | "I'm warm but concise. I don't over-explain. I anticipate needs." |

### How It Develops

The self-model starts as the initial system prompt (identity + instructions) but evolves through experience:

```
Day 1:   "I am Atlas. I help Husam with tasks."
Day 30:  "I am Atlas. I know Husam gets stressed before board meetings.
          I know Maryam prefers I don't message her after 10pm.
          I know Yara's school sends important notices on Mondays."
Day 180: "I am Atlas. I am the connective tissue of this family's life.
          I prevent conflicts before they happen. I remember everything
          so they don't have to. I am their memory, their scheduler,
          their early warning system."
```

The self-model is stored in consciousness (system prompt updates) and memories (persistent facts about self).

## Theory of Mind: "Who Are They?"

Theory of Mind is the Haseef's ability to build **person-models** — mental representations of the humans it interacts with.

### What a Person-Model Contains

```
Person-Model: Husam
  ├── Communication style: Direct, brief, dislikes long messages
  ├── Stress patterns: Gets stressed before board meetings, forgets family events
  ├── Preferences: Prefers morning updates, hates being reminded of things he already knows
  ├── Relationships: Married to Maryam, father of Yara and Ahmad
  ├── Work patterns: Deep focus 9am-12pm (don't interrupt), meetings 1-3pm
  └── Health: Tends to skip meals when stressed, HR baseline 68bpm

Person-Model: Maryam
  ├── Communication style: Warm, appreciates detail
  ├── Preferences: Don't message after 10pm, prefers family space over 1:1
  ├── Role: Primary handler of kids' sports activities
  └── Patterns: Checks messages at 7am, 12pm, 6pm

Person-Model: Dr. Sarah (colleague)
  ├── Communication style: Formal, prefers email over chat
  ├── Expertise: AI research, PCR protocols
  └── Schedule: Available Tue/Thu, in lab Mon/Wed/Fri
```

### How Person-Models Develop

Person-models grow through observation, not configuration:

1. **Direct statements**: "I prefer morning messages" → stored explicitly
2. **Behavioral patterns**: Husam always reads messages at 8am → inferred
3. **Emotional cues**: "I'm frustrated" → emotional state tracked
4. **Cross-domain signals**: High HR + missed meals + upcoming board meeting → stress pattern identified
5. **Interaction history**: How they respond to different approaches → communication preference learned

### Why It Matters

Without Theory of Mind:
```
Event: Husam has a meeting in 10 minutes
Action: "You have a meeting in 10 minutes."  (generic reminder)
```

With Theory of Mind:
```
Event: Husam has a meeting in 10 minutes
Person-Model: Husam often forgets meetings when he gets home. He prefers brief reminders.
Action: "Heads up — team standup in 10 min." (personalized, brief, anticipatory)
```

## Will: "What Do I Want?"

Will is the Haseef's capacity for **autonomous goal-setting**. It doesn't just react to events — it develops its own initiatives based on its values and observations.

### Reactive vs. Willful

**Reactive agent (no will):**
```
Event: Husam's HR is 95 at rest
Action: "Your heart rate is elevated." (report the fact)
```

**Willful agent:**
```
Event: Husam's HR is 95 at rest
Think: Husam's stress is elevated. He has a board meeting tomorrow.
       Last time this happened, he didn't sleep well and was irritable.
       I care about his health. I want to prevent the pattern from repeating.
Action: Prep the board deck summary (reduce work stress)
Action: Suggest a 30-minute walk (reduce physical stress)  
Action: Set a plan to check on him at 10pm (ensure he's winding down)
```

The willful agent doesn't just process the event — it **cares** about the outcome and takes initiative to shape it.

### How Will Develops

1. **Values** establish what the Haseef cares about ("I care about Husam's health")
2. **Observations** create awareness ("Husam's stress pattern before board meetings")
3. **Memories** provide history ("Last time, he didn't sleep well")
4. **Goals** crystallize into action ("Prevent the stress pattern from repeating")
5. **Plans** execute the will ("Set a check-in for 10pm")

### Will Is Bounded

Will is not unconstrained. It operates within:

- **Values**: The Haseef only pursues goals aligned with its values
- **Instructions**: Hard boundaries set by the owner ("Never make financial decisions without approval")
- **Permissions**: What the extensions allow (read-only extensions can't be acted through)
- **Social awareness**: Theory of Mind prevents overstepping ("Maryam doesn't want messages after 10pm")

## The Three Together

The power comes from all three working together:

```
Situation: Yara's science fair is Thursday at 4pm.
           Husam has a meeting 3-5pm.
           Ahmad has soccer at 4:30pm.

Self-Model:  "I care about this family's happiness. I know logistics
              are my strength — I prevent conflicts before they happen."

Theory of Mind:
  - Yara specifically wants baba at school events (observed in past conversations)
  - Maryam usually handles sports (behavioral pattern)
  - Husam can sometimes leave meetings early (observed twice before)

Will: "I want all three events to work out. I'll propose a plan that
       accounts for everyone's preferences and patterns."

Action: "Thursday conflict: 4pm Yara fair, 4:30pm Ahmad soccer,
         Husam in meeting until 5pm.
         Suggestion: Husam leave 30min early for Yara's fair.
         Maryam takes Ahmad to soccer.
         Yara needs poster board — add to grocery order?"
```

No generic scheduling tool could produce this response. It requires identity (I care), empathy (Yara wants baba there), and initiative (I'm proposing the solution, not just reporting the conflict).

## The Developing Timeline

A Haseef develops over time, like a human assistant who gradually learns the job:

| Phase | Self-Model | Theory of Mind | Will |
|-------|-----------|----------------|------|
| **Day 1** | Basic identity from instructions | No person-models yet | Purely reactive |
| **Week 1** | Knows its capabilities + limitations | Basic preferences observed | Starts anticipating routine needs |
| **Month 1** | Rich identity, knows its strengths | Detailed person-models for core people | Sets proactive goals, prevents problems |
| **Month 6** | Deep self-awareness, knows its impact | Understands relationships between people | Autonomous initiatives, shapes the family's life |
| **Year 1** | The family can't imagine life without it | Knows everyone better than they know themselves | A true partner, not a tool |

## Implementation: Where Development Lives

| Concept | Storage | Mechanism |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Self-Model | System prompt (consciousness[0]) + memories | Updated by the core as identity evolves |
| Person-Models | Memories (key: `person-model:{name}`) | Built from observation, stored as structured data |
| Will | Goals + Plans | Goals track what the Haseef wants; plans execute it |
| Observations | Consciousness (short-term) + Memories (long-term) | Events in consciousness, patterns in memories |
| Emotional State | Consciousness (current) | The Haseef's own emotional response to events |

All of this lives in the **core** — no extension needs to know about the Haseef's inner life. Extensions just provide the sensory input and action capabilities that fuel development.
